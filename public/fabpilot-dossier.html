Product thesis

What this product is trying to prove

Core claim

High-stakes AI products need visible trust boundaries. FabTwin Guardian treats evidence, judgment, and action as separate surfaces. That makes the AI feel inspectable rather than magical and keeps approval, accountability, and debugging central to the UX.

User problem

Critical operational context is fragmented across logs, traces, screenshots, SOPs, and human memory. Maintainers and operators lose time before they can decide.

System architecture

The five-layer operating model

01 · Perception

Risk-aware intake

Voice, dashboards, screenshots, lot state, and shift continuity signals all enter the same evidence-first flow.

02 · Evidence

Grounded review pack

Diffs, alarms, SOPs, screenshots, and prior incidents become a navigable evidence graph instead of isolated facts.

03 · Decision

Operator-safe reasoning

Observed facts, ranked hypotheses, and bounded next actions are made visible.

04 · Action

Approval-gated execution

Navigator plans, issue updates, and UI actions stay visibly blocked until the right human boundary is crossed.

05 · Trust

Replay and handoff

Trace IDs, timeline recovery, and signed handoff packs keep the system operable after the live moment ends.

Trust and debugging

Why the system feels engineered, not improvised

Trust surfaces

  • Observed evidence is separate from recommendation.
  • Low-confidence or risky actions stop at human approval boundaries.
  • Every critical action can be narrated, audited, and resumed by the next owner.

Debug surfaces

  • Trace IDs connect event context, screenshots, retrieval, and operator-safe planning.
  • Checkpoint recovery lets operators resume without guessing.
  • Failure taxonomy makes stale context, blocked execution, and uncertainty visible.
